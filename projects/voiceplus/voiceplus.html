Voice Plus

Voice Plus is the traditional channel for phone calls, plus all the other features that Eltropy provides. Not only can agents handle a phone call, they can also do things like send text messages, perform a Remote Check Deposit, or co-browse with a contact while on the call. The ability to use Eltropy features while on a call expands your ability to assist contacts and meet their needs. 

Voice Plus offers all the standard phone call features, such as:

  • Call controls like mute and hold
  • Conference calls
  • Voicemail

It also offers contact center features:

  • Warm and cold transfers
  • Callbacks
  • Supervisor barge-in
  • Call recording and transcripts
  • After-call work (applying tags and notes)

Additionally, you have options for reporting. The Live! dashboard lets users view real-time data, and your Reports and Insights modules let you obtain historical data. 

Artificial Intelligence (AI) with Voice Plus

You have two options for utilizing AI with voice calls:

  • Analyze your voice interactions to generate useful insights
    Along with interactions through other channels, AI Intelligence can analyze your voice calls and produce data about the interaction.
  • Handle voice calls with a voice AI agent
    Voice AI agents can handle the bulk of contacts' simpler issues. This opens up bandwidth for human agents to handle more complex issues. If the AI agent is unable to satisfactorily resolve an issue, the contact can transfer to a human agent.

Voice Call Flows

Voice calls route through your system using a call flow. A call flow is like a phone menu system or IVR. The call flow works with your skill based routing setup to connect incoming callers with the best agent to handle their request. It also lets you add customizations to the phone menu, like offering callbacks for long queue times. You create a call flow in the Call Flow Designer. 

Tip:

Consider what happens when a contact starts a chat through the Conversations Panel. They open the panel, possibly verify their phone number, then select a queue. At this point, your skill based routing (SBR) takes the request and routes it to the most-appropriate agent. The system takes care of the routing according to your SBR setup.

For voice calls, a call flow gives you granular control over this routing experience. You can play messages to the caller, offer a menu system, and so forth.

Contacts Using Private Numbers

Mobile devices often have a setting for the owner to hide their personal information from caller ID. This makes the number Private or Unknown. Eltropy accepts calls from these numbers, and will handle them as anonymous contacts. This means that:

  • Agents start the interaction with a limited feature set
  • Agents need to verify the contact
  • The contact won’t be saved in your system unless the agent verifies their number

International Phone Calls

Eltropy generally supports calls to countries outside the USA. Refer to the International Phone Numbers page for more information.

External Phone Numbers

There are scenarios where you may repeatedly receive calls from an external phone system into your Voice Plus setup, or repeatedly transfer calls to an external system from your Voice Plus setup. For example, a credit card company may frequently send callers over to you to handle issues. Similarly, you may be doing the same; sending callers over to a credit card company. You want to be sure that your Eltropy system handles these scenarios separate from standard calls from contacts:

  • Repeated inbound calls or transfers
    By default, Eltropy treats these like any other inbound call. This is problematic because even though the actual caller is different each time, Eltropy is receiving the transfer from the same phone number. For example, a credit card company's Billing department may repeatedly send callers to you. Your Eltropy system uses phone numbers as the primary identifier for contacts, so the system thinks that it's repeatedly receiving calls from the same contact, just because they're coming from the same Billing department. This causes two problems:
    • The actual caller that is being transferred does not have its own contact entry in your Eltropy system. 
    • This skews your reporting data since individual phone numbers - ie: the Billing department - seem to be making a large number of calls.

    To correct this issue, you can enter these external phone numbers into your Voice Plus settings. Whenever the system receives a call from these external phone numbers, they're treated as anonymous calls. This allows the agent to verify the phone number and enter the contact's details, creating a new contact in your system.
  • Outbound calls or transfers
    In scenarios where your agents are sending callers to a specific phone number outside your Voice Plus system, the external system always receives calls from your Voice Plus phone number handling the call. Also, when transferring calls externally, agents can dial a specific number, or they can select an external number. External numbers display as options if you add them in your Voice Plus settings.

Set Up Voice Plus

Pre-Requisites

  1. Configure the necessary network settings explained on the networking and firewall page. This includes whitelisting the necessary IPs and FQDNs to allow voice traffic.
  2. Procure at least one phone number from the Twilio voice provider. Create a ticket with Eltropy Support or contact your Eltropy representative to assist you with this.

Note:

Before configuring the Twilio number in Eltropy, ensure that necessary features like Audio Management and Workflow Manager are enabled in the Admin Portal with the help of your Eltropy representative.

Step 1: Configure Your Skill Based Routing

A call flow lets you customize what happens when a caller initially calls you, like offering a menu system. In addition to this menu system, the Eltropy system must route the caller to the most-appropriate agent to handle their request. Your skill based routing components allow your system to do so. This includes components like departments, queues, routing profiles, and so on.

If you have an existing SBR setup, you may need to review this setup and incorporate the voice channel.

Review the Contact Routing page to learn about the different SBR-related components. Your SBR setup is unique for your business structure and use-cases, therefore setting up or editing SBR will be different for every domain.

At a minimum, be sure you have the following set up:

  • Departments
  • Queues
  • Hours of operation
  • Routing profiles + proficiencies
  • Conversation permissions

Step 2: Add Countries for International Phone Calls (Optional)

If you want to be able to call – or receive calls – from phone numbers outside the USA, you must add your desired countries in your domain settings.

Note:

Even though Puerto Rico uses the +1 country code, you cannot communicate to phone numbers from Puerto Rico by default. An Eltropy representative must enable Puerto Rico for your domain.

Step 3: Create a Voice Plus Call Flow

A call flow lets you control how a call routes through your system, like a phone menu system or IVR. You create a flow in the Call Flow Designer.

Pre-requisite: Before you create a call flow, review the Call flow designer page. Review the call blocks and what they’re able to do. You may also want to plan your call flow or create a sample diagram.

Follow the instructions here to create a new call flow. You may want to work with your Eltropy representative for guidance. Also, you may want to create the flow in a UAT environment. This will allow you to test the flow and your voice settings before going live.

Be sure to test the call flow.

For example:

A contact calls your support line and selects an option from your IVR menu for loan services. This flow could look like this:

  1. IVR Prompt plays: "Press 1 for Loan Services, Press 2 for Card Services."
  2. Member Presses 1: The call is routed to the Loan Services queue.
    1. Queue Checks for the following.
      • Business Hours: Check if the Loan Services queue is within business hours.
      • Online Agents: Check if there are online agents available.
      • Queue Length: Check if the queue length is less than 3.
  3. Routing: If all checks pass, route the call to an agent.
  4. If the queue is overloaded, offer the contact options to request a callback or leave a voicemail. For this scenario, the AI Agent is not configured in the call flow.

Step 4: Configure Your General Voice Settings

Once you have the phone number configured and the call flows are created, mapping the call flow is necessary for managing the inbound and outbound calls. Once done, you need to configure phone number for inbound calling in the voice settings.

Follow the steps below to configure voice settings.

  1. Navigate to Conversation settings .
  2. Click Voice settings.
  3. Click the General Setting tab.
  4. (Optional) If you want to record all voice calls, enable Voice recording.
  5. (Optional) If you want to skip the pop up that prompts agents to configure their audio, enable Skip incoming call preview. The preview screen lets an agent ensure their audio are configured. Once the agent has configured their audio devices, they typically won't need to do it for every call.
  6. (Optional) If you want to allow agents to make an outbound call to 911:
    1. Enable Emergency Calling.
    2. For Emergency call notification to users, add a phone number that will receive a text message from Eltropy when an agent makes a 911 call. This informs the owner of this phone number that the 911 call was made.
    3. Enter the text message you want to send in Emergency call text template.
  7. Click Save.

Step 5: Add Phone Numbers

You must enter the phone number(s) that you procured from Twilio. This allows your domain to make calls and receive calls using your Twilio numbers.

  1. Navigate to Conversation settings .
  2. Click Voice settings.
  3. Click the Phone numbers tab.
  4. Click .
  5. Add a name for the number, like Collections, Lending, or Contact Center.
  6. Enter the Twilio phone number.
  7. Select your desired inbound and outbound call flows. You can optionally choose Dynamic Routing, which does not use a call flow. Instead, the system routes the phone call like interaction requests from other channels; it uses your skill based routing setup to route the call to the most appropriate agent.
  8. (Optional) Enter an address for your Emergency calling settings. If the agent makes an emergency call, this address is associated with the number, and is therefore accessible to the 911 call handler.
  9. Click Save.

Step 6: Add External Numbers (Optional)

External numbers are for inbound or outbound transfers. You may want to add these numbers in your Voice Plus settings to ensure that the system handles them appropriately.

  1. Navigate to Conversation settings .
  2. Click Voice settings.
  3. Click the External Phone Numbers tab.
  4. Select either Inbound transfer or Outbound transfer, depending on the type of external number you want to add.
  5. Click .
  6. Add name for the external number.
  7. Add the company name.
  8. Add the phone number.
  9. Click Save.

    Note:

    You can also edit or delete external phone number by clicking on the three-dots for an external number.

Call Flow Designer

The call flow designer lets you build an interactive menu system for voice calls. This allows you to customize the contact’s experience for both inbound and outbound voice calls. If call flows are enabled for your domain, you can access the call flow designer from the main navigation bar under Flows.

Key capabilities of call flows are:

  • Providing navigation options so the contact can choose who they want to speak with
  • Offering AI voice bots and other self-service options
  • Allowing multiple calls to occur through a phone number at the same time
  • Generally ensuring that the agent most fit for the issue assists their callers
View a list of typical features of a phone menu system
  • A DTMF menu system that lets callers choose which department they want to speak with by pressing a number. For example, “Press 1 for Sales, press 2 for Technical Support, press 3 for Billing; for all other issues, press 4”.
  • Self-service options that let callers perform basic tasks like resetting an account password, accessing an account balance, or paying a bill. The market is already beginning to adopt AI bots to help contacts accomplish these tasks.
  • “Quality of life” features that make the phone call experience more convenient for callers. This may include on-hold music, holding the caller’s place in the call queue while waiting for an agent, leaving voicemails, offering surveys, and so forth.
  • The ability to pull-in information about the caller from a CRM (or similar) system. This lets the system authenticate callers, display information to agents, and so forth.

Building a call flow is like creating a diagram (the diagram being the call flow). The designer lets you build out each step of the menu system just like a diagram. You drag and drop blocks onto the canvas which you connect one after another. Each block performs a task or action like playing on-hold music, transferring the call, making an API call, and so forth. When the contact calls one of your phone numbers, they start the call flow. Then, the contact moves through the flow block-by-block. When they “arrive” at a block, the block fires and performs its task or functionality.

For instructions on creating and managing call flows, see the Manage Call Flows page.

Key Facts About Call Flows and the Call Flow Designer

  • Call flows are called “projects”.
  • You can create both inbound and outbound call flows. A phone number can be assigned one inbound flow and one outbound flow.
  • After creating a call flow, you assign it to a phone number in Conversation settings > Voice settings > Phone Numbers.
  • You can create call flows for both English and Spanish languages.
  • Single connectors between blocks are called paths. A group of blocks and paths connected together is called a pathway.
  • Call flows are not yet fully integrated with AI capabilities. If you have AI set up for your domain, you can use the IVA Transfer block to send the contact from the call flow IVR to interact with an IVA.

Call Flow Blocks

This section provides information on each call flow block. The blocks are organized into six categories by their purpose and functionality. The following sections explain each block in their correlating category.

Output Blocks

Speak

Plays an audio message to the contact using an AI generated voice. You can enter the text that you want the voice to speak to the contact.

You can delete an existing text to speak by clicking the minus sign next to a text.

Click the Voice - Default button to choose which AI voice you want to speak the text. The default voice is the one you chose in the Default Voice setting when creating the flow.

Tip:

You can add multiple texts to speak. If you do have multiple texts, be sure to connect the paths to and/or from the individual text options and not to the call block overall. See the Call block paths section of this page for more information.

Send SMS

Sends a text message to the contact from a department you specify in this block’s properties. This block has two properties, one to select a specific department and the other to enter the message you want to send to the contact.

Examples of usage:

  • Send a text to a contact after their call finishes with a survey link
  • Prompt a follow-up interaction after a call with a message like How was your experience today?
Media

Lets you play an audio file to the contact. You can upload an MP3 or WAV file from your computer or link to a file stored on the internet. The maximum file size that you can upload is 20MB.

Be sure to consider the following when uploading a media file, especially when the file is used for on-hold music:

Audio played over the telephone network is transcoded to a format the telephone network understands. Regardless of the quality of the file, the system transcodes the file so it plays correctly. This may result in lower quality because the telephone network does not support high bitrate audio. High bitrate, lossy encoded files, such as 128kbps mp3 files, take longer to transcode and potentially sound worse than files that are in lossless 8kbps formats. This is due to the inevitable degradation that occurs when converting from lossy compressed formats and the processing involved in converting from higher bit rates to low bit rates. Playing a file that is longer than 40 minutes can result in a dropped call. If you need to play a file longer than 40 minutes, consider splitting it up into smaller chunks.

Input Blocks

Options

Options lets you present a set of menu options to the caller. They can make a selection with their keypad.

Compared to other similar options like Queues, this block lets you create menus that are more flexible; you can enter the prompt that plays for each option, the button the option is associated with, and so forth. You are not obligated to provide options for 1, 2, 3 buttons in order; you can add options for 1, 2, and 9, for example. You can also add options for the * and # keys.

An example usage of this would be a language menu that says “For English, press one; para español, marca dos”.

Keep the following in mind when using these options:

  • If the DTMF option is configured but not connected to any subsequent block, the menu will be repeated if that DTMF value is selected (applies to * and #)
  • If the No Reply and No Match paths are not connected to subsequent blocks, the menu will be repeated.
  • If you add options for the contact to choose a queue (or department), be sure to connect that option with a Select Queue block then a Transfer to queue block. This is because the options menu only presents a list of options; the other two blocks handle the actually routing of the call.

Tip:

Be careful with using * and # options. To make sure the text-to-speech says exactly what you want to say, you may want to spell out the word instead of using the actual character. For example, "Press star to repeat the menu options".

 
Options Group

Lets you dynamically create a menu of options from a variable value. This block is typically used with the API block. You can make an API call that returns a type of list or set of fields. When that response is captured in a variable, you can reference it in this Options Group block, which presents the options to the contact.

To create a menu with this block:

  1. Make an API call to retrieve a set of options. This must occur in the flow before you want to present the menu.
  2. Capture the response as a variable.
  3. Add an Options Group block in the flow when you want to present the menu.
  4. In the Options Group properties, select the variable with which you captured the API response in the Create callflow options from variable drop-down.
  5. For Label key, enter the JSON key from which you want to generate the menu options. This key should be the name of the object in the returned block of JSON.
For example:
You make an API call that returns a list of external phone numbers. From this list of numbers, you present each phone number as an option in a menu. When the contact makes a selection, you use the selected number in an External transfer block. The system makes an outbound call to the number and transfers the contact out of the system to the external number.
Queues

Queues lets you present a menu of your external queues to the caller. You select which queues you want to populate the menu with, and the block automatically plays the prompt “Press one for <queue 1>, press two for <queue 2>…”. The queues you add as menu options are played in consecutive order, starting with one. This is different from Options, which lets you add options for whichever key you’d like.

This block is similar to Options, however it’s built to specifically provide a menu of queues.

Keep in mind the following when using this block:

  • Be sure to connect each queue option with a path to a Transfer to queue block. This Queues block handles the queue selection and text-to-speech, however you still need to use a Transfer to queue block for the system to move the call into the selected queue. 
  • If you enable Dynamically show available queues in this block's properties, the caller will only be offered queues that have agents online who can handle this queue. This way, they don't select a queue with no agents online, leading to a dead end.

Error Paths:

  • No Reply: This path is taken if the menu prompt plays and the contact does not enter a value during a specified amount of time. Enter the amount of time you want the menu to wait before taking this path. Best practice is to wait 5-10 seconds. You can also choose which path should be taken if the amount of time specified here passes.
  • No Match: This path is taken when the contact presses a key that does not match an option that you’ve configured. For example, if you add three queues, acceptable key presses are 1, 2, or 3. If the contact presses 9, which does not match 1, 2, or 3, this error path is taken.
Voicemail

Sends the contact to an offline queue that you created, letting them leave a voicemail message. The voicemail appears in the inbox of each agent who is assigned the offline queue in their routing profile.

As the contact moves through the call flow paths, when it arrives at this block and this block fires, it first plays an audible beep to the contact, then begins recording the contact-side microphone for the amount of time specified in the Duration (seconds) property. Before this block, you may want to add a Speak block that informs the contact to leave a message after the beep.

In addition to selecting a specific offline queue, you can configure two additional properties:

  • Timeout (seconds): The amount of silence that the system waits for before automatically ending the voicemail message and the call.
  • Duration (seconds): The amount of time that contacts have to record a voicemail.

Additionally, you can continue the call after this block. For example, you may want to use a Speak block that provides one last statement after the contact leaves a voicemail. If you continue the call flow after this block, the call moves to the next block after the amount of time you enter as the duration.

Lastly, a voicemail is not offered for a missed callback request. When the system offers an agent a callback request, if no agents accept it, it goes missed. A voicemail is not offered because callback requests initiate an outbound call to the contact (ie: calling them back); since the contact is not yet engaged on the call, they cannot leave a voicemail.

Advanced Blocks

Condition

Lets you create conditional logic, like an if…else statement. Depending on a certain condition that you set, a certain pathway can be taken as a result. You can add conditions for variables, variable values, logic groups, or expressions.

A logic group lets you create nested conditions using both AND and OR blocks together. Without a logic group you could have only one level of conditions (A and B and C and D). With logic groups, you can use something like (A or B) and (C or D).

An expression lets you directly write the conditions (like a code block) using the logical operators, variables, and brackets. 

For example:
In an Options block, the result path of No Reply leads to a Set block. Here, you set a variable that increments +1 each time the block fires. This Set block leads to a Condition block. The condition is that if the variable is less than or equal to 3, the contact is directed back to the Options menu. If the variable value is greater than 3, the path is taken to play a message that says “We weren’t able to hear your response. Please text us at 8018675309”.

The idea is that the caller has three tries to make a selection in the menu. If the system does not detect a selection three times, it plays the message that prompts the caller to text the business instead.

Set

Lets you set a variable value. You can create a new variable and set its value, or change an existing variable’s value. If you create a new variable with this block, the new variable is automatically added to the Variables list on the left-side of the canvas.

The scope of all variables listed in the left-hand Variables section is global within a flow. You can use and reference any variable within a flow; you cannot reference variables from other flows. The exception to this global scope are any variables that you set in a Javascript block. Variables set in a Javascript block are limited to that specific block.

End

Ends the call.

If any call block does not have a path to another block – if a block is the last in a pathway – the call will automatically end after it finishes firing or functioning. This means that you do not need to explicitly end every pathway that you create. However, best practice is to end a pathway with this End block, making it clear that this is your desired and designed behavior.

Continue waiting

Keeps the contact waiting in a queue. This block is specifically designed to work with an advanced queue options flow. This flow lets you present options to a contact while they're waiting in a queue. For example, they can request a callback, switch to texting, or continue waiting. To continue waiting, the option should connect with this block. When doing so, this loops the queue waiting experience again: Transfer to queue block → wait template text-to-speech → wait music → options while waiting. 

Keep in mind the following when using this block:

  • When this block fires and loops the contact back through the queue waiting experience, the contact does not lose their position in the queue. This ensures that they're able to select a different option while waiting in a queue, like requesting a callback, without losing their spot in line.

Code Blocks

Javascript

Lets you add custom JavaScript code that runs when the block fires.

You can reference any variables created outside the block and are listed in the left-hand Variables section. The scope of all variables listed on the left-hand Variables section is global within a flow. You can use and reference any variable within a flow; you cannot reference variables from other flows. The exception to this global scope are any variables that you set in a Javascript block. Variables set in a Javascript block are limited to that specific block.

API

Lets you make a REST API call. You must enter the full URI, select the verb, then add any headers, parameters, and how you want to capture the response. The API you’re calling must respond with a JSON block. From this block, you must capture the value of a specific JSON key in a variable. In the Capture Response field, use dot notation to specify which field in the response you want to capture. Use the following format: response.<JSON key>. In the Capture Response drop-down, choose which variable you want to set the value to.

You can configure the success and failure result paths. The success path is taken when the API response is able to successfully locate the JSON key you specified. The failure path is taken when the key you specified cannot be found.

Queue Management Blocks

Select Queue

Select Queue lets you create a path that leads to a specific queue. For example, you could add one of these blocks for each queue in an menu created with an Options block. If a contact wants to speak to a sales rep in the Sales queue, you can select the queue, check that sales agents are online, then transfer the contact to the queue with a Transfer to queue block. In this block’s properties, use the drop-down to choose which queue you want to work with in the path you’re creating.

Keep in mind the following when using this block:

  • When you add blocks after this Select Queue block, the subsequent blocks "know" the queue that this block selected. For example, let's say you added this series of blocks: Select Queue → Check queue reps online → Transfer to queue. In this example, the Pony Loans queue was selected. The subsequent two blocks that check for reps then transfer the call keep the context that the Pony Loans queue was selected.
  • You do not need to use this block after a Queues block. This is because the Queues block selects a queue when the contact chooses one of the options.

Tip:

When agents manually transfer a contact from their agent workspace, that is not handled by the call flow; you do not need to build that capability in your call flow.

Check anonymous call

Checks whether the incoming call is from an anonymous contact. An anonymous caller means that you don’t already have the phone number added in your Eltropy system for a contact. You can put this block early in your call flow to split the path between anonymous and known contacts, allowing you to handle them as desired.

Check holiday

Checks whether the current day is a holiday that you’ve configured for your domain. This lets you set up a unique call branch for holidays. For example, if it is a holiday, you can play a unique message to the caller and ask them to try again tomorrow. You can set holidays in your out of office settings.

Check queue business hours

Checks the hours of operation profile assigned to a queue and determines whether the current time is in or out of the profile’s hours of service. The resulting paths are Yes (within hours of service) or No (not within hours of service).

Check queue reps online

Checks whether there are reps online who can handle contacts from a specific queue.

Check queue depth

Checks how many contacts are already waiting in a queue. If you are experiencing high call volume and wait times are long, you can give an incoming contact other options like leaving a message or requesting a callback.

Transfer to queue

Places a caller in a specific queue. This block also handles what happens as the contact is in the specified queue, like playing wait music. This block is actively running for the duration of the call between the agent and contact. If you add a path after this block, the contact will continue along the path only after the call ends.

Tip:

When agents manually transfer a contact from their agent workspace, that is not handled by the call flow; you do not need to build that capability in your call flow.

Settings to configure:

  • Wait Template: Similar to the Speak block, you can use an AI voice to speak a message to the contact. Enter the text here that you want to play to the contact as text-to-speech. For example, you could enter: "Please wait while we find the right agent to assist you". After this message plays, this block then starts playing the Wait Music. After the music finishes, this wait message plays once again. As the contact waits for an agent, the block loops through playing the wait message and wait music. 
    In this setting, you can use specific tags. These tags dynamically insert information into the spoken text. For example, {{.firstName}} would play the contact's first name. You could enter: Thank you for waiting, {{.firstName}}, we'll be with you shortly. Be sure to include the double curly braces and the period (dot notation). The following are tags that you can use for a wait message:
    • firstName: The contact's first name.
    • lastName: The contact's last name.
    • email: The contact's email address.
    • countryCode: The country code of the contact's phone number.
    • mobile: The contact's phone number.
    • domainName: The name of your Eltropy domain.
    • queuePosition: The contact's current position in the queue.
  • Wait Music: Plays music to the contact as they’re in a queue waiting for an agent to answer. You can upload an MP3 or WAV file from your computer, or link to a file stored on the internet. After the file finishes playing, the Wait Template you configured plays to the contact, then loops back to the wait music. Alternatively, if you connected this block to a subsequent block, the next block will fire instead of looping back and forth between the wait template and music.
    If you do not upload or link any music, Eltropy plays a default wait music file. This default wait music is 25 seconds long.
  • Hold Message: When an agent is actively speaking with the contact then places them on hold, an AI voice speaks this message. After the message finishes, the hold music begins playing. This block loops between the hold message and hold music while the contact is on hold.
  • Hold Music: When an agent is actively speaking with the contact then places them on hold, this music plays after the Hold Message finishes. You can upload a music file from your computer or link to a file stored on the internet.
    If you do not upload or link any music, Eltropy plays a default wait music file.
  • Advanced Queue Options: Lets you offer a set of options while the contact is waiting in a queue. This is useful for long wait times; you can let the contact choose a callback, send a text message, leave a voicemail, and so forth. By default, the Wait Template configured in this block plays first, then the wait music. The system-default wait music plays for four minutes (or the wait music you uploaded), then the advanced queue options plays. If the contact continues waiting, this process repeats (Wait Template > Wait Music > Options). If you use the default wait music plays for 1.5 minutes with each subsequent loop before offering the options.
    See the Queue with Options section of the Call Flow Customizations page for more information.
External transfer

Lets you transfer the contact outside of your Eltropy system. For example, you could have a menu option that lets contacts report fraud. The resulting pathway for that option would lead to this block, which contains the phone number for the FTC fraud reporting hotline.

You can transfer contacts externally via phone number or SIP ID (SIP is a protocol commonly used in telephony systems).

When the contact reaches this block and it fires, the contact exits the Eltropy system completely. This means that the contact ends, you cannot produce any further reporting data, they must call again to re-enter your system, and so forth.

IVA Transfer

Transfers the contact to an AI voice agent. To use this block, you must have AI enabled for your domain and an AI voice agent set up. The only accepted property for this block is the AI voice agent's project ID. This is the unique identifier for your AI agent. You can find this identifier at Conversations module > Conversations settings > Voice settings > AI Config tab > Project ID for AI Voice.

Note:
If you use this block in your call flow, your Eltropy representative may need to update the settings for the Voice Plus phone number you use for this call flow. When setting up the phone number, if your Eltropy representative did not add a Call status change webhook in Twilio, they must do so. This ensures that the Interaction List properly tracks the status changes of the call after it's transferred to the AI voice agent.
IVA Transfer v2

Transfers the contact to an AI voice agent (AI Voice 2.0). To use this block, you must have AI enabled for your domain and an AI voice agent set up. The only accepted property for this block is the AI voice agent's ID. This is the unique identifier for the AI agent.

Request callback

Saves a contact’s position in a queue, allowing the contact to drop from the call. When their turn comes up, the system makes a callback call to the contact. 

This block is typically used in combination with Check queue depth. You can check the queue depth, and if there are a certain number of contacts already waiting in the queue, you can offer the incoming contact the option to save their spot and receive a callback.

The Enhanced Callback Options setting in this block lets you offer a set of options to the contact during their callback. See the Callback with Options section on the Call Flow Customizations page for more information.

RONA also applies to callback requests for voice calls. Callback requests allow a contact who is waiting in a queue to stop the call, but the system keeps their position in the queue. When it's their turn, the system offers the callback to an agent. When an agent accepts, the system makes an outbound call to the contact. 

This block also allows you to upload a file that plays wait or hold music. The maximum file size that you can upload is 20MB. Be sure to consider the following when uploading a media file, especially when the file is used for on-hold music:

Audio played over the telephone network is transcoded to a format the telephone network understands. Regardless of the quality of the file, the system transcodes the file so it plays correctly. This may result in lower quality because the telephone network does not support high bitrate audio. High bitrate, lossy encoded files, such as 128kbps mp3 files, take longer to transcode and potentially sound worse than files that are in lossless 8kbps formats. This is due to the inevitable degradation that occurs when converting from lossy compressed formats and the processing involved in converting from higher bit rates to low bit rates. Playing a file that is longer than 40 minutes can result in a dropped call. If you need to play a file longer than 40 minutes, consider splitting it up into smaller chunks.

A call block contains nodes. For example, the screenshot below shows an Options block. Each option in the block is an individual node. The first three DTMF nodes were added in the block’s settings, and the No Reply and No Match nodes are default nodes that are configured in the properties.

Text-to-Speech Effects

Some blocks let you use text-to-speech. This means that you type a phrase in the block properties, and the Eltropy system speaks that sentence when the call block fires. A simple example of this is when you create a menu system with the Options block, and the system speaks "Press 1 to speak with a teller. Press 2 to speak with a loan officer...". 

You can select which effects to add to your text from the Effects drop-down. This adds XML to the text, denoting which words have the effect. You can edit the text + XML to control exactly how you want the effects to apply to the text. 

Remember the following when adding TTS effects:

  • Be sure to include the text that you want to receive the effect in between the opening and closing tags. As shown in the screenshot below, the opening tag indicates that the text will be spoken slowly, and the closing tag is at the end of the sentence, ensuring that the full sentence will be spoken slowly. In other words, the tags are around the full text, so the full text receives the effect.
  • As shown in the screenshot below, the text that will be spoken slowly is black. If the color of the text is light blue, like the color of the tags, then the text you type will not save.

Call Block Paths

The connectors between blocks are called paths. When a contact calls your system and enters the call flow, they’re able to navigate around the network of call blocks that are connected by paths. The only way a contact can move to different blocks is through a path.

When you create a path between blocks, the system automatically creates the shape of the path. If you have a large, complex call flow, this may require you to spread out your blocks to for an easy-to-read structure. You may want to label your paths to simplify your designing experience.

With paths, you can:

  • Connect Call Blocks
    Each option in a block has a dot on the right side. Left-click the dot and drag your mouse to whichever new block or option you want to connect it with.
  • Delete Block Paths
    You can delete a path by clicking the path and pressing the delete key on your keyboard.
  • Label Block Paths
    You can add a label to call paths. This adds text to the mid-point of a path, giving you insight into where. the path leads and what it’s for. Just like call block labels, path labels are purely informational for call flow designers, they do not impact the contact experience or call flow.
    To add a label, left-click a path to select it then click Enter label to begin typing.

Call Flow Versioning and Publishing

As you edit a flow, your changes are automatically saved in the call flow designer. This means you do not need to save your changes as you work. It also means that your changes are only saved in the designer; a call flow is not updated with changes until you publish it. Publishing a call flow creates a new version of the flow with all changes that have been made. You can see and interact with these versions by clicking the Version button in the designer.

Changes are only saved in the designer because the flow might already be in-use. When you assign a flow to a phone number, any time a call happens for that number, the call flow is in-use for that call. The flow designer lets you make and test changes that only exist in the designer. Once you click Publish in the designer, the system creates a new version of the flow and the phone number automatically uses the newest version. In this case, if a call flow is actively running for another contact, that call flow will not change. Only new contacts will use the newly-published call flow.

Call Flow Best Practices

  • You should always check for active agents before transferring to a queue. The only exception is when you use the Dynamically show available queues setting in the Queue block. Other checks are optional and depend on the use case, such as if you want a different message or handling for out of office hours.
  • Select Queue is only needed when using the Options block. When using the Queues block, it will automatically handle this.
  • You can test different AI voices in the AI Config tab of your Voice settings (Conversations module > Conversations settings > Video settings > AI Config).
  • End every pathway with an End block. This makes it clear to others who may edit the call flow – including yourself – what the design is.
  • Create a set of guidelines for your call flows, like a style guide. The guidelines help you structure all your call flows similarly, keeping them organized and uniform even if multiple personnel have access to them. Examples of call flow guidelines:
    • Label each call block according to its function, like Request callback or Present menu of queues.
    • Label each pathway.
    • Color code sets of pathways, like all blocks along the path for each queue in a menu option.
    • Use canvas comments only for questions or suggestions about the call flow.

Create a Call Flow

Required role: Domain Manager

Before creating a call flow, work with your internal teams to understand the experience you want to create for your callers.

  1. Open your UAT domain.
  2. Click the Flows module in the left-hand main navigation.
  3. Click Create.
  4. Name the call flow.
  5. For Modality, select if you want to create an inbound or outbound flow.
  6. For Languages, select which language you want the flow to support. This impacts the voice options and prompts the call flow plays to callers.
  7. Select a Default Voice. This is the voice that speaks audio prompts to the callers. Individual call blocks can override this voice and use a different voice. You can hear what the voices sound like in the AI Config section of the Voice settings (Conversations module > Conversation settings > Voice settings > AI Config).
  8. Click Save. The new flow is added to the list of call flow projects.

Start or Test a Call Flow

Call Flow Customizations

Every financial institution has its own unique needs. Depending on your organization size and structure, the services you offer, and other factors, you may need to tailor your call flows to meet your needs. This page provides example customizations that may help you design your call flows.

Feel free to use these possibilities as inspiration for your own call flow expertise and creativity; this page is not exhaustive for all the potential use cases.

Important!

Be sure to test your call flows extensively to ensure they function appropriately according to your Eltropy system.

Menus

You have multiple options for creating a menu. The two most common options are to use either a Queues or Options block:

  • Queues
    This block is specifically designed to create a menu for contacts to select queues (or to reach certain departments). This is mainly because of its Dynamically show available queues setting. When enabled, if no agents are online to handle a specific queue, then it will not be presented to the contact as an option. Also, because contacts select a specific queue with this block, you do not need to add a Select Queue block after each option.
  • Options
    This essentially lets you create a custom menu. It allows you to use the text-to-speech (TTS) to present any prompt, then a contact can use their keypad to make a selection. Example menus using this block are:
    • Contacts choosing between English and Spanish.
    • While a contact is waiting in a queue, present them options like continue waiting, requesting a callback, switching to text messaging, and so forth. 

You can also use the Options Group block. This block requires advanced configurations that involve API calls, parsing JSON, and so forth, and is less commonly used.

Queue with Options

You can present options to a contact while waiting in a queue. The purpose is to mitigate the time spent waiting. Even if a queue has a long wait time, the contact can possibly solve their issue in other ways. Also, giving the contact options allows them to choose their desired experience, increasing their satisfaction. 

This section explains how to make a queue with the following options:

  • Continue waiting
  • Leave a voicemail
  • Request a callback
  • Switch to the texting channel
  • Connect with a specialist (an external transfer)

Callback with Options

A callback is when a contact is waiting in a queue, and instead of waiting for a long time, the system automatically calls them back when their "spot" reaches the front of the queue. Callbacks are a great way to ease your contact's stress of waiting for an agent. 

How it works:
After requesting a callback, the contact can hang up the phone. The system saves their spot in the queue. When their spot reaches the front of the queue, the system offers a call request to an agent who can handle the queue. When the agent answers, the system then makes an outbound call to the contact. When they answer, they're presented with the option to end the call or speak with an agent.

Expand to view image of an example call flow segment

The following screenshot shows a queue options flow. The branch highlighted in blue is the callback functionality.

Callbacks are generally created in conjunction with queue options, explained in the previous section. While the contact is waiting in a queue, they listen to wait music. After the wait music finishes one loop, a series of options plays. One of which is a callback option. 

To start a callback:

  1. Open an existing call flow in your Call Flow Designer.
  2. Add the Request Callback block to your flow. This is typically preceded by an Options block, where one option is to request a callback. This is illustrated in the previous example segment.
  3. Click the nodes to open the properties panel, then enable the Enhanced Callback Options setting.
  4. Connect the callback block to an Options block.
  5. Add two options: one for connecting with an agent and the other to end the call. Remember, the Options block uses text-to-speech, so consider the prompts that you want spoken to the contact.
  6. Add an End block and connect it with the option to end the call.
  7. Add a Continue waiting block and connect it with the option to speak with an agent. 
  8. Configure the No Reply and No Match nodes of the Options block however you see fit. For example, you could connect them with a Speak block that says Sorry, I didn't catch that, then returns to the options menu.
  9. Test the call flow.

Custom Wait Music

Consider the standard experience of waiting in a call queue:

  1. Contact selects which department they want to talk to
  2. Contact is placed in that department's queue
  3. A text-to-speech prompt is played, and the contact starts waiting for an agent to accept their call request
  4. While waiting, music plays
  5. Loop through steps 3 and 4 until an agent accepts the call

The system-default wait music plays on a 25-second loop. However, you can customize:

  • The music that plays by using your own music file
  • The repeated wait music time in an advanced queue with options

These two customization options are explained in the following sections.

Use a Custom Music File

In the Transfer to queue block, the Wait Music property lets you use your own music. You can upload an MP3 or WAV file, or you can host a file on a cloud server and link to it. If you don't use your own file, the system-default music will play, which repeats every 25 seconds.

Custom Repeat Time for Wait Music

This is applicable when you have an advanced queue with options, explained in the section above. 

You use a Media block to control the amount of time that wait music plays after the first music loop. The first music loop plays with the Transfer to queue block. Then, one of the queue options is to continue waiting. This option should connect to a Media block. In this block, you upload a music file with the duration that you want the subsequent music loops to be. The screenshot below illustrates an example.

In this instance, you could upload a 4-minute music file in the initial Transfer to queue block. Then, in the Media block, you could upload a separate file that's only 1.5 minutes long. This way, the queue options are repeated every 1.5 minutes, if the contact chooses to continue waiting. Additionally, if the contact makes a menu selection while listening to the custom wait music, the call flow recognizes their selection even though the music is playing. 

Call Overflow

During busy times of the day, you may want to offload calls to a third party or contact center outside of your Eltropy system. This may help you reduce certain SLAs like a high average hold time (AHT) and may improve your staffing options since agents may become overwhelmed.

You can accomplish this in the call flow designer. You can use a combination of Check queue depth and External transfer blocks to handle the overflow. For example, you can add a threshold for your queue size. If the queue reaches and surpasses a certain number of callers, the system can start directing the new incoming callers to an external number.

Alternate options:

For high call volume, you can also:

  • Use the Request callback block. This lets the contact hang up the phone so they don’t have to sit and wait. The system holds their spot in the queue and calls the contact when it’s their turn.
  • Use the Voicemail block to transfer calls to an offline queue. This lets the contact leave a message for your agents.

Automate an NPS Survey after a Voice Call

At the conclusion of a call, you can automatically send a text message with a link to a survey. This can be done using the Send SMS block. Add the message you want to send for the survey and include the survey link.
